# Healthcare Data Schema

## Overview

The dataset is a synthetic Electronic Health Record (EHR) generated by [Synthea](https://github.com/synthetichealth/synthea). It models the full lifecycle of patient care — from demographics and clinical events to billing and insurance.

---

## Table Reference

### Group 1: Master Data

| Table | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **patients** | Core entity. Demographics, address, income, and lifetime healthcare costs for each patient. |
| **organizations** | Healthcare facilities (hospitals, clinics) with address and revenue. |
| **providers** | Doctors and care providers, each affiliated with one organization. |
| **payers** | Insurance companies (Medicare, Medicaid, private). Tracks coverage amounts, revenue, and member statistics. |

---

### Group 2: Clinical Events

All clinical tables revolve around `encounters` as their central hub.

| Table | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **encounters** | **Central table.** Each row is one patient visit — a wellness check, emergency visit, inpatient stay, etc. Links patient, provider, organization, and payer together at a point in time. |
| **conditions** | Diagnoses recorded or initiated during an encounter (e.g. hypertension, diabetes). Has a start/stop date to track active vs. resolved conditions. |
| **procedures** | Medical procedures performed during an encounter (surgeries, lab tests, therapy sessions). |
| **medications** | Prescriptions issued during an encounter. Tracks drug, dosage, cost, payer coverage, and reason. |
| **observations** | Measured values recorded during an encounter: vital signs (height, weight, blood pressure), lab results, and survey scores. |
| **immunizations** | Vaccines administered during an encounter. |
| **allergies** | Patient allergies (drug, food, environment). May be linked to a specific encounter when first identified. |
| **imaging_studies** | Diagnostic imaging (X-ray, MRI, CT). Includes DICOM metadata: body site, modality, series, and instance UIDs. |
| **careplans** | Long-term care plans (e.g. chronic disease management, post-surgery rehab). Spans multiple encounters over time. |
| **devices** | Medical devices assigned to a patient (wheelchair, pacemaker, hearing aid). Tracks start/stop dates. |
| **supplies** | Consumable supplies used during an encounter (dental tools, wound dressings). |

---

### Group 3: Financial / Billing

| Table | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **claims** | Insurance claim filed for an encounter. Contains diagnoses, billing status, and outstanding balances across primary/secondary insurers. |
| **claims_transactions** | Line-item transactions within a claim: CHARGEs, PAYMENTs, ADJUSTMENTs, and TRANSFERs. |
| **payer_transitions** | History of a patient's insurance changes over time (when they switched plans or insurers). |

## Key Design Notes

- **`encounters` is the hub.** Almost every clinical and financial table references a specific encounter via `ENCOUNTER` foreign key.
- **`patients` is the second-most universal key.** It appears in nearly every table, allowing full patient history reconstruction without joins through encounters.
- **`ORGANIZATION` is stored on both `encounters` and `providers`** by design — a provider may work at multiple facilities, and the encounter captures exactly where care was delivered at that moment in time (historical accuracy).
- **Codes follow standard medical vocabularies:** SNOMED-CT for clinical concepts, LOINC for observations, RxNorm for medications, and DICOM for imaging.
